Neptune

Overview

Neptune is the fourth and final Jovian planet in the solar system. Like all Jovian planets it is made of gas and has extreme wind speeds, the highest recorded were up to 1340 km/hr in the Great Dark Spot in 1989. Neptune has 14 known moons, the largest of which is known as Triton. Neptune's gravitational pull is the second strongest of all the planets in the solar system, this fact is what led to the discovery of the planet in the first place as the orbit of Uranus was noted as being interfered with by some unseen force (Neptune).

Moons of Neptune

There are 14 moons of Neptune that can be sorted into two categories.

Regular moons − Regular moons are those moons that formed as the planet was forming. These moons are Naiad, Thalassa, Despina, Galatea, Larissa, S/2004 N 1 and Proteus.

Irregular moons − Because of Neptune's strong gravitational pull some large astral bodies were caught in its pull while travelling past the planet and became moons for Neptune. These moons are: Triton, Nereid, Halimede, Sao, Laomedeia, Psamthe and Neso.

Neptune facts

Slow ride − Because of Neptune's distance from the sun its orbit around the Sun is extremely slow, it takes 164 years to orbit the Sun once! As of July 2011 it has only orbited the Sun once since its discovery in 1846.

Cold − The average surface temperature on Neptune is -214 degrees Celsius.

Storms − Neptune has short lived, large scale storms similar to the Great Red Spot found on Saturn.

Missions to Neptune

The Voyager 2 mission is the only mission to have been past Neptune. No other missions are currently planned to go to the planet.
