Kuiper Belt Objects

Overview

The Kuiper belt is a huge band of rock, dust and frozen methane, nitrogen, ammonia and water. It is theorised that the materials in the Kuiper belt are what remains after the formation of the Jovian planets in the outer solar system and as such are a point of interest for scientists to try and grasp the state of the solar system when it was just forming. It is thought that Neptune’s moon Triton and Saturn’s moon Phoebe originated from the Kuiper belt.

Currently, the largest object within the Kuiper belt is the dwarf planet Pluto.

Missions to the Kuiper Belt

The New Horizons mission is currently studying the Kuiper belt after it completed its mission to view Pluto and its largest moon Charon in 2016.
