Planets − Terrestrial vs Jovian

Overview

There are 2 types of planet that all the planets in our solar system can be categorised as: Terrestrial (Earth-like) and Jovian (Jupiter-like).

Terrestrial

  • Small, dense, rocky planets.
  • Located in the inner part of the solar system.
  • Very few moons, if at all.
  • No ring structures.
  • The Terrestrial planets in our solar system are: Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars.

Jovian

  • Large gas giants without a solid surface
  • Located in the outer parts of the solar system.
  • Each Jovian planet has many moons.
  • All Jovian planets have ring systems.
  • The Jovian planets in our solar system are: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.

The exception

There is one exception to this rule, and that is Pluto. Pluto is a hybrid of rock and frozen gasses in the outer areas of the solar system. More information regarding this is covered in the Pluto section.
